TouchBase vs Notion

Dedicated Personal CRM vs All-in-One Workspace

Overview

While both TouchBase and Notion can be used for relationship management, they take fundamentally different approaches. TouchBase is a dedicated personal CRM with purpose-built features, while Notion is a versatile workspace tool that can be customized into a CRM using templates. Let's explore how these different approaches compare.

Notion

Detailed Analysis

User Interface & Experience

TouchBase provides a streamlined, purpose-built interface designed specifically for relationship management. It's intuitive and requires no setup - you can start adding contacts and setting reminders immediately. Notion, while highly customizable, requires significant initial setup and ongoing maintenance of your CRM system. You'll need to either create your own template or import one, then learn how to use and maintain it effectively.

Features & Capabilities

TouchBase excels with its relationship-focused features like AI-powered insights, intelligent reminders and automated rescheduling of missed interactions. The app's conversation prompts and personalized suggestions help maintain meaningful connections. Notion offers unlimited flexibility in how you structure your CRM, but lacks specialized features for relationship management. While you can create custom views, automations and reminders, they require manual setup and maintenance.

Mobile Experience

TouchBase's mobile apps are fully featured, making it easy to log interactions and check reminders on the go. Notion's mobile experience is more limited, primarily focused on viewing and basic editing. Complex operations like filtering, sorting and customizing views are better suited to desktop use.

Pricing & Value

TouchBase offers better value for dedicated relationship management with its $3/month premium plan, which includes all features including AI capabilities. Notion's Plus plan at $10/month provides unlimited blocks and file uploads, but you'll need to invest time in setting up and maintaining your CRM system. While Notion's free plan allows unlimited contacts through templates, TouchBase's free plan is limited to 15 contacts but includes core relationship management features.

Who Should Choose Each Platform?

Choose TouchBase if you:

  • Want a dedicated relationship management tool
  • Value AI-powered insights and automation
  • Need a robust mobile experience
  • Prefer a ready-to-use solution
  • Want intelligent reminders and follow-ups

Choose Notion if you:

  • Need unlimited customization options
  • Want to integrate CRM with other workflows
  • Don't mind manual setup and maintenance
  • Prefer working primarily on desktop
  • Already use Notion for other purposes

Final Verdict

While both TouchBase and Notion can serve as personal CRMs, they cater to different needs. TouchBase is the better choice for users who want a dedicated, efficient relationship management tool with smart features and minimal setup. Its focused design, AI capabilities and mobile-first approach make it ideal for active relationship management.

Notion is better suited for users who want complete control over their CRM setup and prefer to integrate relationship management into a broader workspace. However, this flexibility comes with the trade-off of requiring more time for setup and maintenance.
